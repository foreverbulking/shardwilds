# Shardwilds Game Vision

## One-Line Pitch

Shardwilds is a colorful browser-based sandbox action MMO where players explore a charming open world, fight monsters with fast stylized combat, gather resources, farm land, craft goods, run trade packs, enter solo/co-op dungeons, and compete over dangerous high-value regions.

## Core Inspirations

Shardwilds takes inspiration from three directions:

1. Colorful adventure RPG charm
   - Bright fantasy worlds
   - Memorable monsters
   - Cozy adventure tone
   - Readable silhouettes
   - Playful heroic energy

2. Action MMO combat feel
   - Fast third-person movement
   - Exaggerated attack animations
   - Readable skill effects
   - Dodges, combos, and satisfying impact

3. Sandbox MMO world systems
   - Open-world life skills
   - Trade routes
   - Farming
   - Crafting
   - Regional economies
   - Player housing later
   - Contested resources
   - PvP risk
   - Player-driven stories

The game must remain original. It must not copy names, monsters, characters, music, UI, lore, or protected visual designs from any reference.

## Original Identity

Shardwilds is not a pure theme park MMO where players only queue dungeons and chase gear scores.

It is a living fantasy world where players create stories through gathering, crafting, trading, combat, farming, exploration, cooperation, and conflict.

The world should feel charming and approachable, but still have meaningful risk in the wilderness.

## Target Player Fantasy

The target player fantasy is:

> I live in a colorful fantasy world where I can adventure, craft, trade, build a home, run dangerous trade routes with friends, fight monsters, enter dungeons, and occasionally risk PvP for better rewards.

## Core Pillars

### Pillar 1: Charming Adventure

The world is colorful, readable, playful, and full of strange creatures, cozy towns, hidden caves, resource spots, dungeons, and small stories.

### Pillar 2: Action Combat

Combat is real-time and animation-driven. Players move with WASD, aim with the camera, dodge attacks, use skills, and fight enemies with readable telegraphs.

### Pillar 3: Life Skills Matter

Fishing, mining, farming, cooking, alchemy, blacksmithing, and crafting are not side chores. They feed the economy, trade routes, consumables, gear, housing, and dungeon preparation.

### Pillar 4: Sandbox Economy

Materials have regional value. Players gather, refine, craft, transport, sell, and risk goods across the world. Trade packs and regional demand create player stories.

### Pillar 5: Controlled Risk

PvP exists, but not everywhere. Safe zones protect casual players. Dangerous zones offer higher rewards. Trade routes, rare resources, and contested regions create meaningful conflict.

## MVP Definition

The first MVP is called Hearthvale Online.

Hearthvale Online includes:

- Browser client
- Third-person movement
- Stylized player character placeholder
- Multiplayer presence
- Nameplates
- Local chat
- Server-authoritative position validation
- Inventory
- Mining node
- Fishing spot
- Basic farming plot
- One crafting station
- One trade pack route
- Persistence through refresh

The MVP does not include:

- Full combat classes
- Huge open world
- Auction house
- Ships
- Housing
- Guilds
- Large-scale PvP
- Complex dungeon bosses
- Procedural world generation
